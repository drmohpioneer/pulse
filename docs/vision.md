# Pulse Vision

Version: 1.0

---

# Why Pulse Exists

Cardiac arrest is one of the most cognitively demanding situations in medicine.

Within seconds, a clinical team must coordinate multiple parallel tasks while making life-critical decisions under extreme pressure.

The team leader is responsible for simultaneously:

- Leading the resuscitation
- Interpreting cardiac rhythms
- Tracking medications
- Tracking CPR cycles
- Monitoring timing
- Considering reversible causes (H's & T's)
- Coordinating communication
- Planning the next intervention
- Documenting the event

These responsibilities create significant cognitive load.

Although experienced clinicians are trained to manage this complexity, the environment remains chaotic, time-critical, and mentally demanding.

Pulse exists to reduce this cognitive burden.

---

# The Problem

Today's resuscitation tools are primarily timers and documentation aids.

They record what happened.

They do not continuously understand what is happening.

Clinicians must mentally maintain the current state of the resuscitation while simultaneously treating the patient.

This creates unnecessary mental workload.

---

# Our Vision

Pulse is an AI Clinical Teammate.

It continuously estimates the current clinical state of a resuscitation from multiple sources of evidence.

It maintains situational awareness.

It tracks the evolving clinical picture.

It reminds clinicians of time-critical interventions.

It surfaces clinically relevant information at the right moment.

Most importantly, it reduces cognitive load without disrupting existing clinical workflows.

---

# What Pulse Is Not

Pulse is not:

- a chatbot
- a CPR timer
- a documentation application
- a replacement for clinicians
- an autonomous decision maker

Pulse supports clinicians.

It does not replace them.

---

# Core Belief

The future of medical AI is not replacing doctors.

The future is building trusted AI teammates that help clinicians perform at their best during the most demanding moments of patient care.

Pulse aims to become one of those teammates.

---

# Long-Term Goal

Create an AI system that clinicians trust because it is:

- transparent
- reliable
- clinically useful
- secure
- evidence-driven
- aware of uncertainty

Success is not measured by how much Pulse talks.

Success is measured by how much mental workload it removes from the resuscitation team.

---

# Product Principle

Every new feature must answer one question:

> Does this reduce cognitive load during cardiac arrest?

If the answer is no, the feature should be reconsidered.

## Product Principle

Pulse is not a documentation tool.

Pulse is a real-time cognitive support system.

Its purpose is to continuously answer one question:

"What is the most important thing the resuscitation team should do next?"

## Product Principle

Pulse continuously answers one question:

> Given everything that has happened so far, what is the most important thing the team should do next?

Everything else in the system exists to answer that question accurately.
